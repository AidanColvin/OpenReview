## What Rayyan Does

Rayyan is a web and mobile app. It helps researchers sort through thousands of scientific papers. This process is called a systematic review. Researchers use these reviews to find the truth in massive amounts of data. Doing this by hand takes months. Rayyan speeds this up. It uses a mix of human choices and smart software.

Purdue University researchers describe it clearly. "Rayyan is a free, online application to assist researchers with systematic review methodology and meta-analysis projects." 

Think of a systematic review as searching for a needle in a massive haystack. Rayyan acts as a strong magnet. It pulls the right papers to the top. It lets you toss the bad ones away fast.

## How Rayyan Works

Rayyan acts like an assembly line for research. You feed it raw data. It helps you sort it into a finished product.

* **Step 1: Upload Data.** You export search results from databases like PubMed. You upload them into Rayyan using standard text files.
* **Step 2: Remove Duplicates.** The software finds exact matches. It deletes papers that show up twice.
* **Step 3: AI Screening.** You start reading titles and summaries. You label them "Include" or "Exclude". The AI watches your choices. A study in the National Library of Medicine notes: "Throughout the citation screening process, Rayyan offers suggestions for article inclusion based on the authors' prior selections." The system learns your rules. It brings the best matches to the top.
* **Step 4: Collaborate.** You invite other users. You read the same papers. You make choices without seeing their votes.
* **Step 5: Resolve Conflicts.** If you say "Include" and your partner says "Exclude", the system flags it. You talk it out and pick a final answer.
* **Step 6: Extract Data.** You pull the exact numbers or text from the final papers. You put them into a clean chart inside the app.

## Why People Like It

People like Rayyan because it is easy to use. The design is simple. It does not look cluttered. 

It also has a mobile app. This is a huge benefit. For example, a student can read and sort papers on their phone while riding the bus. They do not need to sit at a desk. It turns wasted time into work time.

## Why People Use It

Researchers use Rayyan because it enforces strict rules. A good review must be blind. This means one reader cannot see what the other reader chose. This stops bias. Rayyan forces this blind mode. It keeps the research pure. 

It also handles massive data files well. Reviewers can upload over 100,000 records. The software does not crash or slow down.

## What Makes It Helpful

Rayyan takes away the "Excel nightmare." In the past, teams used giant spreadsheets. They got lost. They made mistakes. 

Rayyan tracks every single click. It knows who made a choice and when. It acts like a digital referee. It keeps everyone organized. The built-in chat and labels let teams talk inside the app. They do not need to send confusing emails back and forth.

## What Can Be Improved

Rayyan is not perfect. There are clear flaws.

* **Weak Customization:** Once you set up labels, it is hard to change them later.
* **AI Limits:** The AI suggests papers, but it does not do the reading for you. It is a helper, not a replacement. Its ability to find bad papers is sometimes low.
* **Cost Spikes:** It is great for single users. However, the price jumps very high for large teams or schools.
* **Not All-in-One:** It is best for the early sorting phase. It is not as strong for the deep, final data analysis compared to a tool like Covidence.

## Free Version vs. Paid Version

Rayyan offers different tiers. 

**The Free Tier**
* **Cost:** $0.
* **Target:** Students and beginners.
* **Limits:** You can only have 3 active reviews at a time.
* **Team Size:** You can invite up to 2 free reviewers.
* **Features:** Basic duplicate removal and standard sorting filters.

**The Paid Tiers (Essential and Advanced)**
* **Cost:** Ranges from roughly $60 to $100 per year for individuals.
* **Target:** Small teams and serious researchers.
* **Limits:** 9 active reviews or more, depending on the plan.
* **Team Size:** You can share seats with 5 to 10 reviewers.
* **Features:** Paid plans get AI-powered data extraction. They get an auto-resolver for duplicates. They can also create diagrams automatically.

The free tier is like test-driving a car. It gets you down the road. The paid tier is like hiring a driver. It handles the heavy traffic for you.
